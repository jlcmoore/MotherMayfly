Mother Mayfly

A mixed-media installation that births emphemeral poetry.

When a user pulls the cord…

…Mother Mayfly prints computer written poems…

…or Mother Mayfly prints human written poems…

…that expire like a mayfly in the heat.

Artist's Statement

Ephemera:

  1. a mayfly, esp one of the genus Ephemera
  2. something transitory or short-lived

Mother Mayfly is, then, that which births ephemera1. In so doing, Mother Mayfly crosses divides. It explores what occurs between human-computer, physical-digital, fun-serious, fleeting-lasting, and literal-metaphoric. It is a computer, but has a pull-cord instead of a screen (physical-digital). The ease of use welcomes play, but the poems present dark images of human life (fun-serious). Poems written by laureates and beats appear next to those written by an artificial intelligence (human-computer). Its children are poems that have lasted since antiquity (like those of Catullus) and ones that exist only on the small strips of paper that encourage crinkling and fade in the sun (fleeting-lasting). The installation is divided (literal-metaphoric) by a board cut from a cherry tree some meters away and which had grown with generations of community-based farmers, but the board holds up plastic imported from Asia, code sourced from all of Siliconia, and sits beneath a hole that connects to a power grid fuelled equally by natural gas and coal (local-global).

  1. The name is completely unrelated to the phrase "Mother may I?" and does not at all comment on the subordination inherent in the human-computer divide.
